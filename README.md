## I. The Vision: From Vapor to Earth

Modern digital infrastructure is "Vapor-based." It relies on the "Cloud"—a euphemism for centralized servers owned by a handful of billionaires. When the provider pulls the plug, the ecosystem evaporates.

**Guncelium** is an **Earth-based** network. It is a digital mycelium that lives in the "soil" of the user's own hardware. By bridging **Tor**, **Gun.eco**, and high-speed local transports (**WebRTC, BLE, Wi-Fi P2P**), we are building a network that doesn't just survive in the dark—it thrives there.

## III. Technical Architecture (The Mycelium Stack)

Guncelium utilizes a "Hybrid Transport" logic to bridge the global and local worlds:



1. **The Hyphae (Tor):** Anonymous, thread-like connections for global signaling. Like fungal threads in the deep woods, they allow peers to find each other without being seen by the "pesticides" of state surveillance.
2. **The Pheromones (Bluetooth BLE):** Low-energy beacons used for proximity discovery. Offline devices broadcast their "scent" (metadata spores) to find nearby neighbors.
3. **The Rhizomorphs (Wi-Fi Direct / WebRTC):** Once a connection is made, devices trigger high-speed "expansion tunnels" to sync the global graph at native speeds.
4. **The Gateway Bridge:** A device connected to the "Internet" (the air) pulls global spores down and "injects" them into the local, offline root system.
